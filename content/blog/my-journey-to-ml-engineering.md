# My Journey from Physics Olympiad to ML Engineering

Looking back at my career path, it's fascinating how my physics background laid the foundation for my work in machine learning. Here's my story.

## The Physics Foundation

My journey began at Luong Van Chanh High School for The Gifted, where I developed a deep passion for physics and problem-solving. Competing in the Vietnam National Physics Olympiad taught me invaluable skills:

### Problem-Solving Mindset
Physics olympiads aren't just about memorizing formulas—they're about:
- Breaking down complex problems into manageable parts
- Finding elegant solutions under constraints
- Thinking creatively when standard approaches fail

These skills translate directly to ML engineering, where we constantly face novel challenges that require innovative solutions.

### Mathematical Rigor
Physics demanded strong mathematical foundations:
- Linear algebra for electromagnetic fields
- Calculus for mechanics and thermodynamics
- Statistical mechanics connecting to probability theory

Later, I realized these are the exact same tools used in machine learning!

## The Transition to Engineering

At HCMUT, I studied Control Engineering and Automation. This was my first real exposure to:
- **Signal Processing**: Understanding data in different domains
- **System Optimization**: Finding optimal control policies
- **Modeling**: Creating mathematical representations of real-world systems

My thesis on "Positioning and navigation solution for automated guided vehicle using QR code" combined computer vision, control theory, and practical engineering—a perfect bridge to ML.

## Breaking into AI

### First Steps: AI VIET NAM (2022-2023)

As an AI Researcher at AI VIET NAM, I dove deep into:
- Computer vision projects (classification, detection, style transfer)
- Building neural networks from scratch
- Understanding ML/DL theory fundamentally

This was crucial—I didn't just learn to use frameworks; I understood *why* they work.

### Embedded Systems Interlude

A 3-month stint at Viettel High Tech teaching me:
- Low-level programming (Yocto Project, embedded Linux)
- System constraints and optimization
- Hardware-software interfaces

This experience proved invaluable later when optimizing ML models for production.

### Focusing on NLP & LLMs

At VietAI (Dec 2023 - Jan 2025), I specialized in Large Language Models:
- Research in natural language processing
- Exploring state-of-the-art LLM architectures
- Understanding transformer models deeply

Simultaneously at Delta Cognition, I applied this knowledge to real-world products:
- Building production LLM systems
- Implementing RAG architectures
- Optimizing for latency and cost

## Current Work: Trusting Social

Now as an ML Engineer at Trusting Social, I'm:
- Working on cutting-edge LLM applications
- Building robust RAG systems
- Solving challenging problems at scale

## Key Lessons

### 1. Strong Fundamentals Matter
My physics and math background helps me understand ML at a deeper level than just API calls.

### 2. Problem-Solving > Domain Knowledge
The ability to tackle novel problems (learned from olympiads) is more valuable than knowing specific tools.

### 3. Breadth Complements Depth
My diverse experiences (embedded systems, CV, NLP) give me a unique perspective on ML problems.

### 4. Learning Never Stops
From physics olympiads to LLMs, staying curious and continuously learning is essential.

## Advice for Aspiring ML Engineers

If you're starting your journey:

1. **Build Strong Foundations**: Linear algebra, calculus, probability—invest time here
2. **Code from Scratch**: Implement algorithms yourself before using libraries
3. **Work on Real Projects**: Theory is important, but building real systems teaches you differently
4. **Stay Current**: The field moves fast—follow papers, blogs, and discussions
5. **Find Your Niche**: Explore different areas (CV, NLP, RL) to find what excites you

I'm excited about:

- Contributing to open-source ML tools
